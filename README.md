# Heart Attack Prediction (case study: *Indonesia*)

## Project Overview

Heart disease is one of the leading causes of mortality worldwide, and early risk prediction plays a crucial role in prevention and healthcare planning.  
This project focuses on **analyzing and predicting heart attack risk among individuals** using a comprehensive dataset that combines medical, behavioral, and demographic factors.

The goal is to **explore patterns, identify key risk factors, and provide insightful visual analytics** that support data-driven decision making in healthcare.
🔗 **Dataset Source:** [Link](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ankushpanday2/heart-attack-prediction-in-indonesia)
***

## Dataset Description

The dataset used in this project was collected from Kaggle and contains a rich set of attributes covering multiple dimensions that affect heart health.

**Dataset highlights:**

*   **Rows:** 158,355 individuals
*   **Columns:** 28 features + target label
*   **Target column:** Indicates whether the individual experienced a heart attack (`Yes / No`)

### Categories of Features:

*  The dataset contains 28 features representing demographic, clinical, lifestyle, and medical screening information.
Below is a detailed description of each column:
``Demographic Information``

age: Age of the individual in years
gender: Biological gender of the individual
region: Geographical region within Indonesia
income_level: Socioeconomic income category of the individual


``Medical & Clinical Risk Factors``

hypertension: Indicates whether the individual has high blood pressure (Yes/No)
diabetes: Indicates presence of diabetes (Yes/No)
cholesterol_level: Overall cholesterol classification (Normal / High / Very High)
obesity: Indicates whether the individual is clinically obese (Yes/No)
waist_circumference: Waist measurement used as an obesity indicator
family_history: Presence of heart disease in family history (Yes/No)


``Lifestyle & Behavioral Factors``

smoking_status: Smoking behavior (Never / Past / Current)
alcohol_consumption: Level of alcohol intake
physical_activity: Physical activity intensity (Low / Moderate / High)
dietary_habits: Quality of diet (Healthy / Moderate / Unhealthy)
stress_level: Stress classification (Low / Moderate / High)
sleep_hours: Average number of sleep hours per day


 ``Environmental & Social Factors``

air_pollution_exposure: Level of air pollution exposure
participated_in_free_screening: Participation in free medical screening programs (Yes/No)



## 📈 Project Scope & Analysis

This project mainly focuses on:

*   Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
*   Statistical comparison between **heart attack vs non–heart attack** groups
*   Visual exploration of major health risk factors
*   Identifying high‑risk populations based on age, habits, and medical conditions

### Key analytical questions:

*   How do age and gender affect heart attack risk?
*   What is the impact of **diabetes, hypertension, and high cholesterol**?
*   How lifestyle habits such as **smoking, stress, and physical activity** influence outcomes?
*   Does medication usage reduce heart attack risk?

***

## 🧠 Key Insights

Some important insights derived from the analysis include:

1.  Patients with **diabetes and high cholesterol** show the **highest heart attack risk**.
2.  **Elderly individuals with hypertension** have a significantly higher incidence rate.
3.  Heart attacks can still occur even among individuals with **normal blood pressure**, indicating multi-factor influence.
4.  Lifestyle factors such as **smoking and stress level** strongly correlate with increased risk.
5.  Medication usage shows a measurable impact on reducing severe outcomes.
